## Chapter 1 - Present Simple: Daily Routines and Habits

**Grammar Focus:** Present Simple affirmative, negative, yes/no, and wh-questions with do/does and third person singular
**Speaking Goal:** Describe and ask about routines and habits

### Grammar
- Formation: base verb (+s/es for 3rd person)
- Regular verbs: work → works, study → studies
- Irregular: have → has, do → does, go → goes
- Spelling rules for 3rd person
- Time expressions: every day, on weekdays, at night

### Key Vocabulary
- Daily actions: get up, take a shower, get dressed, have breakfast/lunch/dinner, go to work, study, work, cook, watch TV, go to bed
- Time expressions: every day, on weekdays, on weekends, in the morning, at night, once a week, twice a month
- Quantifiers: some, a lot of, many (questions/negatives), few, a few

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** I get up at 7 am every day.
- **Negative:** I don't watch TV at night.
- **Yes/No:** Do you go to work on weekends?
- **Wh:** What time do you have breakfast?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. I get up at the crack of dawn (very early)
2. I hit the ground running (start energetically)
3. I call it a day (finish work)
4. I'm an early bird (wake up early)
5. I'm a night owl (stay up late)
6. I burn the candle at both ends (work too hard)
7. I'm swamped (very busy)
8. I'm tied up (occupied)
9. I'm running behind schedule (late)
10. I'm ahead of schedule (early)
11. I'm on the clock (working)
12. I'm off the clock (not working)

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **get up** - wake up and rise
2. **wake up** - stop sleeping
3. **go out** - leave home for entertainment
4. **come back** - return
5. **stay in** - remain at home

### Pronunciation Focus
- Third person -s: /s/, /z/, /ɪz/
- "Does" weak form: /dəz/
- Sentence rhythm in routines
- Weak forms: "at" → /ət/

### Communication Practice
- Describing daily routines
- Comparing schedules with partners
- Talking about work/school habits

## Chapter 2 - Present Simple vs Present Continuous: Contrast

**Grammar Focus:** Differences in use and structure (simple with do/does, continuous with am/is/are + verb-ing)
**Speaking Goal:** Identify and use the correct tense based on context

### Grammar
- Simple markers: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never
- Continuous markers: now, right now, at the moment, today, this week
- Combined actions: work, study, travel, eat, read, cook, play, clean

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** I usually work in the office, but today I'm working from home.
- **Negative:** I'm not studying right now; I study in the evenings.
- **Yes/No:** Are you working now? / Do you work on Saturdays?
- **Wh:** What are you doing right now? / When do you usually study?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. Business as usual (normal routine)
2. Same old, same old (nothing new)
3. It's just another day at the office
4. I'm in my element (doing what I enjoy)
5. I'm in the zone (focused)
6. I'm on autopilot (doing without thinking)
7. I'm going through the motions (without enthusiasm)
8. It's par for the course (normal/expected)
9. I'm up to my ears in work (very busy)
10. I'm snowed under (overwhelmed)
11. I'm spinning my wheels (working without progress)
12. I'm chasing my tail (busy but unproductive)

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **work out** - exercise, solve
2. **hang out** - spend time casually
3. **chill out** - relax
4. **calm down** - become calm
5. **focus on** - concentrate

### Pronunciation Focus
- Contrast stress: "I WORK" (simple) vs "I'm WORKing" (continuous)
- Continuous -ing: /ɪŋ/
- Contextual intonation differences

### Communication Practice
- Contrasting habits vs current actions
- Explaining exceptions to routines
- Context-based tense selection

## Chapter 3 - Likes, Activities, and Frequency

**Grammar Focus:** like/love/hate + verb-ing, adverbs of frequency, How often...?
**Speaking Goal:** Express preferences and talk about frequency

### Grammar
- Preference verbs + gerund: like reading, love dancing
- Frequency adverbs: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never
- Frequency expressions: once/twice/three times a week

### Key Vocabulary
- Activities: play soccer, read books, listen to music, go shopping, watch movies, go out with friends, swim, cook, travel, spend time with family, have fun

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** I like reading books in the evening.
- **Negative:** I don't like going shopping on weekends.
- **Yes/No:** Do you like cooking?
- **Wh:** How often do you go swimming?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. It's not my cup of tea (not my preference)
2. I'm not a fan of (don't like)
3. I'm really into (enthusiastic about)
4. I'm crazy about (love very much)
5. I can't get enough of (really enjoy)
6. I'm hooked on (addicted to)
7. I'm not big on (don't particularly like)
8. It grows on you (become to like over time)
9. I'm sick of (tired of)
10. I've had it up to here with (very annoyed by)
11. I'm fed up with (frustrated with)
12. I could take it or leave it (indifferent)

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **get into** - become interested in
2. **take up** - start a hobby
3. **give up** - stop doing
4. **cut down on** - reduce
5. **get bored of** - lose interest

### Pronunciation Focus
- "Like" /laɪk/ + gerund linking
- Frequency adverb stress
- Preference intonation

### Communication Practice
- Discussing hobbies and interests
- Comparing preferences

## Chapter 4 - Family, Descriptions, and Adjectives

**Grammar Focus:** Possessives (my, your, his, her, our, their), Saxon genitive, adjectives and their order
**Speaking Goal:** Describe people and family relationships

### Grammar
- Possessive adjectives and pronouns
- Saxon genitive: John's car, my parents' house
- Adjective order: opinion + size + age + color + noun

### Key Vocabulary
- Family: mother, father, parents, sister, brother, cousin, uncle, aunt, grandparents
- Physical adjectives: tall, short, young, old, thin, heavy
- Personality adjectives: friendly, funny, smart, kind, shy

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** My sister is a tall, friendly woman.
- **Negative:** My cousin isn't very tall.
- **Yes/No:** Do you have any brothers?
- **Wh:** Who is the youngest in your family?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. He/she is the spitting image of (looks exactly like)
2. He/she is a chip off the old block (like parent)
3. They run in the family (common trait)
4. Blood is thicker than water (family first)
5. Like father, like son
6. The apple doesn't fall far from the tree
7. He/she is the black sheep (different from family)
8. We're two peas in a pod (very similar)
9. He/she wears the pants (makes decisions)
10. We're like chalk and cheese (very different)
11. He/she is the life of the party (entertaining)
12. He/she has a heart of gold (very kind)

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **take after** - resemble a family member
2. **grow up** - become adult
3. **look after** - care for
4. **bring up** - raise children
5. **settle down** - start stable life

### Pronunciation Focus
- Possessive 's: /ɪz/, /z/, /s/
- Adjective stress patterns
- Family vocabulary linking

### Communication Practice
- Describing family members
- Comparing family traits
- Family tree discussions

## Chapter 5 - Places, Directions, and Surroundings

**Grammar Focus:** there is/are (affirmative, negative, questions), prepositions of place, quantifiers, relative pronouns (who, which, that)
**Speaking Goal:** Describe and locate places, give simple directions

### Grammar
- THERE BE structure review
- Prepositions: next to, in front of, behind, between, across from
- Relative pronouns for descriptions

### Key Vocabulary
- Common places: supermarket, park, school, library, post office, restaurant, bus station, hospital, bank, cinema
- Prepositions of place
- Quantifiers: some, any, a lot of, many, few, little

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** There is a park next to my house. / She is the woman who works at the bank.
- **Negative:** There aren't any restaurants near here.
- **Yes/No:** Is there a supermarket in your neighborhood?
- **Wh:** Where is the post office?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. It's right around the corner (very close)
2. It's within walking distance
3. It's off the beaten path (unusual location)
4. It's in the middle of nowhere (remote)
5. It's a stone's throw away (very near)
6. I'm lost (confused about location)
7. I know it like the back of my hand (very familiar)
8. It's hard to find
9. You can't miss it (easy to find)
10. It's out of the way (inconvenient location)
11. It's centrally located
12. It's in a prime location

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **turn left/right** - change direction
2. **go straight** - continue forward
3. **head for** - move toward
4. **look for** - search
5. **find out** - discover location

### Pronunciation Focus
- Preposition reduction: "next to" → /nekstə/
- Direction stress patterns
- Place name pronunciation

### Communication Practice
- Giving and following directions
- Describing neighborhoods
- Location-based conversations

## Chapter 6 - Plans, Desires, and Abilities

**Grammar Focus:** Catenative construction (want to, need to, hope to, plan to), modal verbs (can, would like), expressing purpose (for + noun, to + infinitive)
**Speaking Goal:** Talk about plans, abilities, desires, and make polite requests

### Grammar
- Catenative verbs + to-infinitive
- Can for ability and permission
- Would like for polite requests
- Purpose expressions

### Key Vocabulary
- Common activities: go to the gym, visit friends, study English, work on projects, travel, learn new things
- Objects and purposes: ticket (for the concert), money (for lunch), plan to travel

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** I can play the guitar. / I plan to visit my family next month.
- **Negative:** I can't swim. / I don't want to work on Saturday.
- **Yes/No:** Can you drive? / Would you like some tea?
- **Wh:** What do you need to buy? / Why are you studying English?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. I have my heart set on (really want)
2. I'm dying to (really want to)
3. I'm itching to (eager to)
4. I wouldn't mind (would like to)
5. I'm up for (willing to)
6. I'm game for (willing to try)
7. It's on my bucket list (want to do before dying)
8. I've always wanted to
9. I can hardly wait (very excited)
10. I'm counting down the days
11. The sooner, the better
12. I'm all for it (strongly support)

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **aim for** - target, strive for
2. **hope for** - wish for
3. **long for** - desire strongly
4. **push for** - advocate for
5. **set out to** - begin with purpose

### Pronunciation Focus
- "Want to" → /wɑnə/ (wanna)
- "Going to" → /ˈɡʌnə/ (gonna)
- Modal weak forms

### Communication Practice
- Making plans and invitations
- Discussing abilities and skills
- Polite requests and offers
