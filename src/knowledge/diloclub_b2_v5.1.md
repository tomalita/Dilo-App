## Chapter 1 - Third Conditional: Past Hypotheticals

**Grammar Focus:** Third Conditional (If + Past Perfect, would have + past participle)
**Speaking Goal:** Talk about hypothetical situations in the past

### Grammar
- If + Past Perfect, would have + pp
- Could have / might have + pp
- Should have + pp (regret/advice)
- Wish + Past Perfect

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** If I had studied, I would have passed.
- **Regret:** I wish I had known.
- **Should have:** I should have listened.
- **Negative:** If she hadn't left, we wouldn't have met.

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. Hindsight is 20/20
2. If only I had known
3. I should have seen it coming
4. I could kick myself
5. I really dropped the ball
6. That was a close call
7. I missed the boat
8. I let the opportunity slip
9. I shot myself in the foot
10. I put my foot in my mouth
11. I really messed up
12. I wish I could turn back time

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **would have put off** - postponed
2. **could have given up** - abandoned
3. **should have looked into** - investigated
4. **might have held on** - waited
5. **would have ended up** - resulted

### Pronunciation Focus
- Would have / would've: /ˈwʊdəv/
- Could have / could've: /ˈkʊdəv/
- Should have / should've: /ˈʃʊdəv/

### Communication Practice
- Expressing regrets
- Discussing missed opportunities
- Alternative history conversations

## Chapter 3 - Advanced Passive & Causative

- All tenses in passive
- Causative: have/get + something + past participle
- It is said that... / He is said to...

### Model Sentences

## Chapter 4 - Relative Clauses: Defining and Non-Defining

**Grammar Focus:** Defining and non-defining relative clauses with who, which, that, whose, where, when
**Speaking Goal:** Add complex information to sentences

### Grammar
- Defining (no commas): The man who called me
- Non-defining (commas): My brother, who lives in Paris, is a doctor.
- Prepositions in relative clauses
- Reduced relative clauses

### Model Sentences

- **Non-defining:** My car, which is very old, broke down.
- **Whose:** The man whose car was stolen reported it.
