## Chapter 1 - Present Perfect: Life Experiences

**Grammar Focus:** Present Perfect (have/has + past participle) for experiences without specific time
**Speaking Goal:** Talk about life experiences without specifying when

### Grammar
- Formation: have/has + past participle
- Ever/never for experiences
- Yet/already/just for recent actions
- Been vs gone distinction

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** I've been to Paris. / She's never eaten sushi.
- **Negative:** I haven't seen that movie.
- **Yes/No:** Have you ever been to Japan?
- **Wh:** What countries have you visited?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. Been there, done that (experienced already)
2. I've been around the block (experienced)
3. I've seen it all
4. I've been through a lot
5. I've had my fair share
6. I've paid my dues (earned respect)
7. I've been in your shoes
8. I've learned the hard way
9. I've been down that road
10. I've had enough
11. I've reached my limit
12. I've hit rock bottom

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **have been to** - visited
2. **have gone to** - gone and still there
3. **have on** - be wearing
4. **have over** - host guests
5. **have out** - eat at restaurant

### Pronunciation Focus
- Have/has contractions: /əv/, /əz/
- Past participle stress
- Experience question intonation

### Communication Practice
- Sharing life experiences
- Comparing travel histories
- Experience-based conversations

## Chapter 2 - Present Perfect with Duration: For and Since

**Grammar Focus:** Present Perfect with for (duration) and since (starting point)
**Speaking Goal:** Describe actions that started in the past and continue to now

### Grammar
- For + period: for 2 years, for a long time
- Since + point: since 2020, since I was young
- How long...?
- Present Perfect vs Past Simple

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** I've lived here for 5 years. / I've known her since 2019.
- **Negative:** I haven't seen him for ages.
- **Yes/No:** Have you worked here long?
- **Wh:** How long have you studied English?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. For as long as I can remember
2. Since day one
3. It's been ages
4. Time has flown by
5. It feels like forever
6. It seems like only yesterday
7. Where has the time gone?
8. It's about time
9. For the time being (temporarily)
10. In the meantime
11. Meanwhile
12. In due course (eventually)

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **keep on** - continue
2. **carry on** - continue
3. **go on** - continue
4. **drag on** - continue too long
5. **hold on** - wait

### Pronunciation Focus
- For weak form: /fər/
- Since stress
- Duration intonation

### Communication Practice
- Discussing long-term activities
- Relationships and friendships
- Career and study duration

## Chapter 5 - Future Perfect & Future Continuous

**Grammar Focus:** Future Perfect (will have + pp) and Future Continuous (will be + verb-ing)
**Speaking Goal:** Talk about actions completed or in progress at a future time

- Future Continuous: will be + verb-ing
- By + future time

- **Negative:** If you don't study, you won't pass.

## Chapter 8 - Passive Voice: Present and Past

**Speaking Goal:** Describe actions when the agent is unknown or unimportant

- Present Passive: am/is/are + past participle
- Past Passive: was/were + past participle
- By + agent (when known)
- It is said that... / He is said to...

### Model Sentences

## Chapter 10 - Gerunds and Infinitives

**Grammar Focus:** Verbs followed by gerunds, infinitives, or both with different meanings
**Speaking Goal:** Use gerunds and infinitives correctly in conversation

### Grammar
- Verb + gerund: enjoy, finish, mind, suggest
- Verb + infinitive: want, need, decide, plan
- Verb + both: remember, forget, stop, try
- Different meanings with gerund vs infinitive

### Model Sentences
- **Gerund:** I enjoy reading. / He finished working.
- **Infinitive:** I want to leave. / She decided to stay.
- **Both:** I stopped smoking (quit) vs I stopped to smoke (pause).

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. I can't help it (can't control)
2. I can't stand it (hate)
3. I can't bear it (intolerable)
4. I don't mind
5. It's worth a shot
6. It's no use (pointless)
7. There's no point in
8. It's no good
9. What's the use?
10. I can't afford to
11. I can't wait to
12. I'd rather not

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **feel like** - want to
2. **fancy** - want (UK)
3. **care for** - like
4. **look forward to** - anticipate
5. **get used to** - become accustomed

### Pronunciation Focus
- -ing pronunciation: /ɪŋ/
- To-infinitive linking
- Gerund vs infinitive stress

### Communication Practice
- Expressing preferences
- Decision discussions
- Habit and change conversations
