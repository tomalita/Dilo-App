## Chapter 1 - Past Simple with Regular and Irregular Verbs

**Grammar Focus:** Past Simple (affirmative, negative, yes/no, wh-questions) + pronunciation of -ed (/t/, /d/, /ɪd/)
**Speaking Goal:** Narrate trips and experiences, answer and ask questions

### Grammar
- Regular verbs: base + ed
- Irregular verbs: common list (go-went, see-saw, eat-ate)
- Time markers: yesterday, last week, ago, in 2020
- Pronunciation of -ed endings

### Model Sentences
- **Affirmative:** I visited London last year.
- **Negative:** I didn't visit London last year.
- **Yes/No:** Did you visit London last year?
- **Wh:** Where did you go last summer?

### Idiomatic Expressions (12)
1. Those were the days (nostalgic)
2. Way back when (long ago)
3. Once upon a time (story opening)
4. In the good old days
5. I remember it like it was yesterday
6. Time flies (time passes quickly)
7. It feels like ages ago
8. That takes me back (brings memories)
9. As far back as I can remember
10. Back in the day
11. When I was your age
12. I've come a long way (improved)

### Phrasal Verbs (5)
1. **look back** - remember
2. **think back** - recall
3. **go back** - return
4. **date back** - originate
5. **bring back** - cause to remember

### Pronunciation Focus
- -ed endings: /t/, /d/, /ɪd/
- Irregular verb stress patterns
- Past time expression intonation

### Communication Practice
- Narrating past experiences
- Sharing travel stories
- Past event discussions

## Chapter 7 - Comparisons, Descriptions & Indirect Objects

- Comparative: -er, more + adjective

## Chapter 10 - How Long Things Take & Participial Adjectives

**Grammar Focus:** It takes (past/present/future), participial adjectives (-ed / -ing)
**Speaking Goal:** Tell how long something takes to get done

- It takes + time + to + verb
- -ed adjectives (how you feel): bored, interested, excited
- -ing adjectives (how something is): boring, interesting, exciting

### Model Sentences

### Phrasal Verbs (5)

3. **slow down** - reduce speed
4. **hurry up** - move faster

### Pronunciation Focus
